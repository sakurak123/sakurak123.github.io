THE LIE interferes with colonial legacies using photography and the photo-book to reflect on their implications in the present.

Andre Bradley lives and works in Philadelphia, PA. Bradley's artistic, curatorial, and photo book practices currently explore the subject of Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs) and the black community. He utilizes mixed and digital media, photography, and installation as forms of ideological resistance that foreground lived experience, adjacent the background of art. Bradley graduated from the Maryland Institute College of Arts, Curatorial Practice program, Image Text Ithaca, and the Rhode Island School of Design's Photography MFA programs.
